📦 How to Sell on Etsy & Gumroad

You have the product. Here's how to get paid.

Option 1: Etsy (Best for Volume)

  1. Go to etsy.com/sell → create shop (free, 5 minutes)
  2. Click "Add a listing" → upload your generated file as the digital download
  3. Title: use the keywords from your product description. Example: "Notion Budget Template | Personal Finance Tracker | Expense Planner for Notion"
  4. Price: $3-15. Start lower ($5) until you get 10+ sales and reviews, then raise.
  5. Tags: use all 13 tag slots. Every tag = a search someone might type.
  6. Photos: create 5 simple mockups using Canva (free). Show the template in use on a laptop/phone.
  7. Cost: Etsy charges $0.20 per listing + 6.5% transaction fee. No monthly fee.

Option 2: Gumroad (Simpler, Better Margins)

  1. Go to gumroad.com → create account (free)
  2. Products → New Product → upload your file
  3. Set price. Gumroad charges 10% flat (no listing fee).
  4. Copy your Gumroad link and share on Reddit, Twitter, Pinterest.
  5. Advantage: No listing fee. Better for testing which products sell before committing to Etsy.

🚀 Quick Wins to Get First Sales

  • Reddit: Post in r/Notion, r/Productivity, r/personalfinance, r/resumes — share a screenshot, offer a discount code in comments
  • Pinterest: Create 5 pins per product. Pinterest is Etsy's #1 traffic source for digital products.
  • Bundle: After creating 3-5 products, bundle them at $19.95 (perceived as great value)
  • Seasonality: Budget templates sell in January. Planners sell in December. Resume templates sell year-round but spike in spring.
